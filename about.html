About

Gabriella Kauffmann is a renowned Danish visual artist whose paintings navigate the intersection between the intuitive and the conceptual. Her works go beyond aesthetic contemplation - they demand presence.

With a background in both artistic practice and embodied perception, Kauffmann creates pieces that resonate with something deeply human. We spoke with her about her artistic approach, her creative process - and her perspective on the role of art in a world in constant flux.

Experiences

Gabriella Kauffmann er en anerkendt dansk billedkunstner, hvis malerier balancerer mellem det intuitive og det konceptuelle. Hendes værker rækker ud over det æstetiske og kræver nærvær fra beskueren.

Med en baggrund i både kunstnerisk praksis og kropslig erkendelse skaber Kauffmann værker, der taler til det dybt menneskelige. Vi har mødt hende til en samtale om hendes kunstneriske tilgang, hendes værkproces - og om kunstens betydning i en verden i konstant forandring.

Passion

Art has always been a constant in my life, but it was only when I felt a profound need to create with both body and mind that I fully embraced a professional artistic practice.

My background as a reflexologist has taught me a great deal about sensory awareness, energy, and presence - all of which I continue to carry into my studio. In fact, art and healing share a fundamental commonality: they are both about connection, about being present in the moment, and about listening to what cannot always be expressed in words.

My paintings stem from a curiosity about the connections and energetic fields we are part of - both visible and invisible. I often work in mixed media and incorporate three-dimensional elements to create depth and a sense of physical resonance in the works.
